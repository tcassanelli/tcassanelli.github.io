About Me

About Me

I'm a 3rd year PhD student at the David A. Dunlap Department of Astronomy & Astrophysics at the University of Toronto, Canada. I am also affiliated with the Dunlap Institute for Astronomy & Astrophysics. Prior to starting my PhD, I completed an Astrophysics MSc at the Universität Bonn and Max-Planck-Institut für Radioastronomie, Germany; as well as an Applied Physics and Mechanical Engineering BSc at Universidad de La Frontera, Chile.

Research

My primary lines of research are Fast Radio Burst 💥 localization, Very Long Baseline Interferometry, radio astronomical instrumentation, and holography for radio telescopes. My PhD thesis is focused on the detection of FRBs with VLBI with a 3000 km baseline from British Columbia to Ontario using the telescopes: Canadian Hydrogen Intensity Mapping Experiment (CHIME) and the Algonquin Radio Observatory (ARO). I belong to LWLab at U of T and to the CHIME/FRB Collaboration.
